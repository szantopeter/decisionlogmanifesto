Manifesto for Decision Logs

We are uncovering better ways of documenting decisions by doing it and helping others do it. Through this work we have come to value:

Evaluating alternatives over choosing the default option
Business value over doing things for the joy of doing it
Cost-benefit analysis over over politics and personal preferences
 Comparision table over lengthy essay
 Central decisions registry over storing decisions in code or email
 Re-evaluating decisions over fear of challenging the status-quo

That is, while there is value in the items on the right, we value the items on the left more.

Peter Szanto Gabor Nagypal
